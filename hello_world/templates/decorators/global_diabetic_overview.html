Global Diabetes Overview

Diabetes is a global epidemic with impactful statistics that underscore its prevalence and the critical need for awareness and education. According to the World Health Organization, an estimated 422 million adults were living with diabetes in 2014, compared to 108 million in 1980. The global prevalence of diabetes among adults over 18 years of age has risen from 4.7% in 1980 to 8.5% in 2014.

Diabetes prevalence has been rising more rapidly in middle- and low-income countries. It is a major cause of blindness, kidney failure, heart attacks, stroke, and lower limb amputation.
